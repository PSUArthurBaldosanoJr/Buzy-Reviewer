UX Laws

UX (User Experience) laws are a set of principles that help designers create user-friendly and intuitive interfaces. These laws are based on psychology and behavioral science to improve user interaction and satisfaction.

Key UX Laws

  • Hick’s Law: The more choices a user has, the longer it takes for them to make a decision.
  • Fitts’s Law: The time to acquire a target is a function of the distance and size of the target.
  • Miller’s Law: The average person can only keep about 7 (±2) items in their working memory.
  • Jakob’s Law: Users spend most of their time on other sites, so they expect your site to work the same way.
  • Law of Proximity: Elements that are close together are perceived as related.
  • Law of Similarity: Users group similar-looking elements together.
  • Law of Common Region: Elements located within the same boundary are perceived as related.

Principles of HCI

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) design principles guide the development of effective and user-friendly systems. These principles are essential for improving usability, accessibility, and user satisfaction.

Eight Golden Rules of HCI

  • Strive for Consistency: Maintain uniformity in design elements.
  • Enable Shortcuts: Provide shortcuts for experienced users.
  • Offer Informative Feedback: Keep users informed about system status.
  • Design for Closure: Ensure users understand when a task is completed.
  • Prevent Errors: Design to minimize the chances of user errors.
  • Permit Easy Reversal: Allow users to undo actions easily.
  • Support User Control: Keep users in control of their interactions.
  • Reduce Memory Load: Present information clearly to reduce cognitive strain.

Lesson 4

HCI Guidelines

HCI guidelines are specific rules that designers follow to achieve usability and accessibility. These guidelines ensure a standardized and effective approach to interface design.

Key HCI Guidelines

  • Consistency: Maintain internal and external consistency across applications.
  • User Control and Feedback: Provide clear feedback to users and allow them to control interactions.
  • Use of Metaphors: Utilize familiar real-world metaphors for ease of understanding.
  • Direct Manipulation: Encourage intuitive interaction through direct object manipulation.
  • Aesthetic Design: Prioritize visual clarity and balance in screen layouts.
